Technology Investment Readiness.

A diagnostic for evaluating whether an emerging technology is ready for the kind of capital you're proposing to deploy — and what the binding constraint is if it isn't.

The problem this framework addresses

TRL is a useful but insufficient measure. A technology can sit at TRL 7 with strong demonstrations and still be unfundable, because its market signal is weak, its team can't operate at scale, or its capital path is mismatched to the available investors. Conversely, a TRL 4 technology with a credible team, a clear customer, and a path to bankable scale can absorb significant strategic capital.

This framework separates the four dimensions that determine whether a technology is actually investable, scores each, and identifies the binding constraint.

The four dimensions

1. Technical Readiness

Beyond TRL. Scored across:

  • Demonstrated performance at relevant scale and duty cycle
  • Quality and independence of validation data
  • Identified failure modes and their mitigation paths
  • IP position — granted patents, freedom-to-operate, trade secrets
  • Replicability — can someone else build this, given the documentation that exists today?

2. Market Signal

Does the world want this, and at what price?

  • Identified customers with named pain points and budget
  • Signed LOIs, MOUs, or paid pilots
  • Defensible pricing analysis — what does the alternative cost, and what's the value gap?
  • Regulatory environment — enabling, neutral, or actively hostile
  • Competitive landscape and time-to-incumbent-response

3. Team Capability

Can this team build and operate what they're proposing?

  • Founder/leadership track record in the relevant domain
  • Engineering depth vs. complexity of the technical task ahead
  • Commercial, regulatory, and capital-markets capability
  • Ability to attract and retain operational talent
  • Board and advisor strength relative to the path ahead

4. Capital Path

Does the funding strategy match the technology and stage?

  • Total capital required to first commercial unit
  • Sequencing — equity, debt, grants, strategic, sovereign
  • Match between investor type and risk profile at each stage
  • Dilution and control trajectory
  • Exit or liquidity logic for each capital class

Scoring

Each dimension is scored 1–5 with explicit criteria for each level. The overall readiness is not an average — it is dominated by the lowest score. A 5/5/5/1 technology is not investment-ready; it is investment-ready except for one binding constraint that must be addressed first.

Output

The diagnostic produces:

  1. A four-dimension score with rationale
  2. An identified binding constraint and the work required to lift it
  3. A recommended capital class and stage match
  4. A list of specific de-risking milestones for the next 12–18 months

How to use it

This framework is most useful in three settings:

  • Investor diligence — to structure a first-look evaluation of a deeptech opportunity.
  • Founder self-assessment — to understand which dimension to prioritize before the next raise.
  • Program design — for accelerators, sovereign funds, and corporate ventures shaping technology pipelines.

Limitations

This is a diagnostic, not an oracle. It surfaces the right questions; it does not answer them. The scoring rubrics still require judgment, and that judgment improves with sector-specific calibration.

I'm working on sector-specific calibrations for hydrogen, critical minerals, water, and energy storage — these will be released as separate framework extensions.
