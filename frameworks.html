Frameworks

Methods I've built
and use with operators, founders,
and investors.

These are the structured approaches I return to most often in my own work. They're shaped by what I've found missing in the commercialization and TEA literature — practical, defensible, and built to be argued with.

01

Technology Investment Readiness

A multi-dimensional diagnostic that scores a technology across technical maturity, market signal, team capability, and capital path — used to evaluate deeptech investment opportunities and identify the binding constraint.

→ 02

Pilot Success Framework

How to design pilots that generate decision-grade data rather than marketing demonstrations. Covers scope, scale, instrumentation, and success criteria — and the failure modes that make most pilots inconclusive.

→ 03

Deeptech Commercialization Path

A stage-gated map from lab milestone to first commercial unit, sequenced by capital, capability, and customer engagement — not by TRL alone. Built for founders and program managers running real timelines.

→ 04

Techno-Economic Analysis Method

A structured approach to building TEAs that survive scrutiny: from feedstock and process boundary definition, through capex and opex disaggregation, to LCOE/LCOH and IRR with defensible uncertainty bounds.

Note

Each framework on this page is a living document. I update them as new evidence comes in or as I encounter situations that break the framework. If you've used one and have a critique or an extension to propose, I want to hear it — get in touch.
