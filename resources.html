Resources

Tools, templates,
and curated reading.

A working library of the spreadsheets, datasets, and external references I find myself returning to. Everything here is either built or vetted by me — and updated when the underlying material moves.

Tools & Templates

Spreadsheet

TEA Template — Process Plant

Structured Excel template implementing the TEA framework. Includes mass/energy balance, capex disaggregation, opex, LCOE/LCOH, sensitivity, and Monte Carlo.

 Spreadsheet

Pilot Design Workbook

Scoping tool for designing pilot programs with decision-grade outputs. Covers scale, duration, instrumentation, and success criteria.

 PDF / Diagnostic

Investment Readiness Scorecard

Printable scorecard for the four-dimension investment readiness diagnostic. Use for founder self-assessment or investor diligence.

 Python Notebook

Learning Curve Calculator

Jupyter notebook for fitting and projecting technology learning curves with confidence intervals. Includes datasets for solar, batteries, and electrolysis.

Datasets

CSV

Hydrogen Plant Capex Benchmark

Compiled capex data for 60+ green hydrogen projects (2018–2025), with disaggregation by stack vs balance-of-plant where disclosed.

 CSV

DLE Project Tracker

Tracking sheet of announced direct-lithium-extraction projects globally, with technology pathway, scale, location, and status.

 CSV

Sovereign Deeptech Fund Database

Sovereign and national funds active in deeptech investment, with mandate, ticket size, and notable portfolio companies.

Curated Reading

Book

Where Is My Flying Car? — J. Storrs Hall

Provocative argument about why deeptech progress slowed in the 1970s. Reading this changed how I think about energy intensity as a development metric.

 Report

IRENA — Renewable Power Generation Costs

Annual benchmark publication. Methodologically careful and the best free source for global capex and LCOE data on mature renewable technologies.

 Paper

Nemet (2006) — Beyond the Learning Curve

The paper that disaggregates "learning" into its mechanistic drivers. Essential reading for anyone using learning curves in TEA.

 Newsletter

Construction Physics — Brian Potter

Best contemporary writing on why building hard things is hard. The "FOAK" essay alone is worth subscribing for.
