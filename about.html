What I work on

My work spans three connected modes:

Analysis — I build techno-economic models for emerging technologies, evaluating the levers that determine cost, scale, and competitiveness. Most of my publications and advisory engagements live here.

Strategy — I advise founders, corporate development teams, and government bodies on commercialization paths: where to pilot, what to license vs. build, how to sequence capital raises, and how to read the strategic logic of incumbents.

Frameworks — I write methods and tools that other people can use. These are publicly available in the Frameworks section. They're shaped by what I've found missing in the commercialization literature.

How I write

The writing on this site is meant to be useful. It tries to combine the rigor of an analyst with the directness of someone who's spent time inside operating companies and labs. I aim for short claims, defensible numbers, and arguments that can be taken apart by a skeptical reader.

If you find a mistake, a missing factor, or a stronger counter-argument — please reach out. The work gets sharper with that kind of pressure.
